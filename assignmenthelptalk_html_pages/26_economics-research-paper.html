How to Write an Economics Research Paper

If you are an Economics student, you are familiar, or you should brace for writing an economics research paper. How to Write an Economics Research Paper?  Well, don’t punic if you don’t know where to start.

This guide will take you through an effective economics research paper’s important features on how to write a perfect paper. It will help if you read along so that you can get started with your research paper.

What does an Effective Economics Research Paper Entail?

A constructive economics research paper should illustrate your academic proficiency in the subject of study. Your primary goal is to convince your reader, in this case, the professor, that you can critically interpret literature while crafting questions based on economics logic. You will also demonstrate skills in sorting out emerging real-time issues.

Economics research should clarify economic reciprocations, providing evidence on your argument while citing sources of study. A good research paper must entail statistical analysis, a vital aspect of economics, and techniques of constrained optimizations.

Types of Economics Research Papers

There are two main types of research papers in economics though there is a thin line between the two in matters variation. In this section, we will highlight different types of economics research papers. This will help you understand the specifics of a particular type of paper for you to write effectively. Here are the types of research papers in economics.

Empirical Research papers.

An Empirical research paper is a type of paper written based on observation or evidence from research on experiments rather than logic.   It depends on data collected to conclude a particular topic.  Empirical research papers are ideal where information is available.  Here you are asked to do research based on observation while answering well-defined questions that are supportive or against the hypothesis. To achieve this perfectly, you need to employ qualitative technics. 

It is important to note that there are two types of empirical research.  It is either you do experimental or non-experimental research.  When carrying out experimental empirical research, you need to use specific proceedings to deliver a well-figured presentation. Non- experimental empirical research is also known as informal research. Here you only need to observe the case of study without any intervention. 

Theoretical Research Paper

Theoretical Economics research papers don’t contain experimental information on the subject of study.  When writing this type of paper, you need to refer to an existing or new abstract principle concerning a specific study subject.  You don’t have to document every evidence of study; instead, you can only highlight critical and interesting proofs when writing a theoretical economics research paper. 

You, however, need to explain your finding after your analysis; write this in the results section of your paper. The most efficient and effective way to show your results is by using tables. You can also use pictorial illustrations to present your results. You should explain the reach, impact, and implications of the theoretical research when writing your paper. In a nutshell, a theoretical research paper logically explores a system of beliefs.

 It is possible to combine the type of research when writing your paper.  Here you can make an assumption then use data to support or disapprove of the theory.  The two processes relate as it requires you to apply some empirical aspects when you study and cite the theory. 

How to Write an Economics Research Paper?

The process of writing an economics research article starts with researching the topic of study.  Doing proper research will make you establish a deeper and wide understanding of the topic. This will help you in developing ideas for the paper while giving you leverage when articulating your ideas.  Research also helps in developing a thesis statement.

Thesis Statement

A thesis statement is a summary of the key points in your economics research article. The statement is critical as it arts as it guides the professor and outlines your presentation’s direction by highlighting the main points and supportive statements or ideas.

At the initial stages of your writing, you need to gather all the relevant materials to facilitate the writing of an authentic piece. Materials like newspapers, government reports and publications, Google Scholar, and other online databases are great sources of critical information that will help you in your college or university work.  After getting the right sources, the next step is to review your sources as you fish for your economics research write-up ideas. As you gather your ideas, keep track of your reference points; you will need to cite your sources in your paper; always remember this.

Once you gathered tons of points and ideas, the next move is to organize your work before you start the process of transferring your ideas into a meaningful document.  Organizing your points gives your research paper focus; hence your idea will be flawless.

It will be best to use a clear outline to organize and present your points and ideas logically.  Once you develop a perfect thesis statement, you can think of a clear and suitable outline for your economics research paper.

All information should be organized in subheadings. Kindly remember that you are writing on important ideas; hence you only need to fit all the key ideas into your outline less they will be irrelevant. Also, remember to focus on your thesis statement leaving out unnecessary information as you write your paper. With a clear outline and thesis statement, you can start your writing. 

An Outline of a Good Economics Research Paper

If you are reading to this point, it means you are having some difficulties in coming up with a desirable outline for your project. This section will take you through an outline of a typical economics research paper. A good paper should have an introduction, body, and conclusion. Here is a highlight of what is expected in each segment of your write-up. 

Introduction.

Writing a paper without an intro is like driving with all tires flat, total madness for the luck of a better term.  An introduction gives your work context and serves as the background of your paper. Start your intro with a strong statement to leave your reader with no option other than reading through your paper. Avoid writing a boring and dry introduction; otherwise, the lecturer will lose interest in your piece. A good introduction should highlight what the paper is about and give the lecturer enough reason to read through your piece.  Furthermore, it should outline the steps to be taken.

To set a context, you need to include important information on your main ideas as it plays a central role in making the professor understand the claims and topic of discussion.  You should outline your main idea stating why you think it matters; be creative when articulating your points; your Economics research paper needs to interest your readers.  This segment allows you to explain to the lecturer why it is crucial to read or care about your research paper. It is critical to creating a compelling and straightforward that give readers the feeling of reading more while act on your research. Briefly state your claims and declare your stand on the topic of study.

Body. 

The body is the main segment of your paper. At this point, your outline should guide you; however, you need to be creative.  It is important to stick to your thesis as you write in an open-minded way.  You need to start with a strong point of your idea. The supportive point is stronger to the point.

You should outline your data you are writing on as you state your information sources. It will help if you highlight strengths, weaknesses, and the time frame of your data collection. In most cases, the questions in the review are not new. It helps if you summarize and analyze other theories in your area of study, relating them to your write-up. 

Conclusion. 

It is important that you wrap up your research paper. Ending your work with a restatement of your thesis statement is not a bad idea. Spice your conclusion by giving a short explanation of how your points support your work, pointing out what needs to be researched further if there are some gaps in your work. 

Once you are done with your writing, you need to go through your piece. Revising your work helps you to detect typos. You will be able to know whether your work is concise with your thesis statement.

Going through the article will make you know if your paragraphs flow in a logical sequence and if your ideas are concrete. Revision allows you to make necessary changes on work if there are issues with language and other factors that make your points not flow.  Check on spelling, punctuation, and grammar errors to make your work clear. 

Writing an economics research paper might sometimes be a hectic task, but reading this piece has helped make it easy for you to write a flawless paper and make your course interesting.
